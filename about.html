About Us

The Dogecoin Foundation is a non-profit organization created by the founders of Dogecoin for the purpose of spreading the use of the currency through goodwill and charitable endeavors. We also provide incentives for developers to work on projects that will enhance the experience of using the coin.

Our Mission Statement

The Dogecoin Foundation is a venture designed to help empower community spirit and improve the user experience. We are not a steering committee that decides and guides the fate of dogecoin, we are simply a conduit and escrow service for projects that help the community and which would not otherwise generate a financial reward for the creator.

We pledge to:

Provide a reliable and trustworthy medium
Users can donate to various causes and development projects and be sure that their coins are going to a legitimate fund.

Provide unbiased listings
If a cause or project meets our criteria, then we will list it. We will make no attempt to influence what the community should and should not support; the amount of doge donated is the true measure of the popularity or support an initiative has.

Take no fee or commission from donations
All donations sent to the foundation will stay in their respective wallet addresses and will not be touched until that fund is paid out in full to the person or persons with the rightful claim to it.

Provide a transparent service
The good thing about dogecoin is that this is inherent in the currency to begin with! Anyone can check the transaction history of all the wallet addresses we list at dogechain.info.

Respect the wishes of the community
With so many shibes it's inevitable that not everyone is going to have the same opinion on everything. We will however do our best to respect and respond to the general wishes and concerns of the community at large on any issue.

Listing And Payout Criteria

Charities

To be eligible to be listed among our charities, your organisation must:

  • Accept donations in dogecoin, and make this clear on the regular donation page of your website.
  • Meet ONE (1) of the following criteria:
    • Be recognized as a charity or non-profit organisation by the taxation laws of the country in which it operates.
    • Be able to otherwise reasonably prove to the Foundation and community that donations are being used in the manner in which they are pledged.

Development Bounties

For a development bounty to be listed it must meet ALL (6) of the following criteria:

  • Another finished product must not already exists that fulfils the main function of the proposed project.
  • The finished product must be open source.
  • The finished product must be available to all members of the community free of charge.
  • The finished product does not directly generate income for the creator e.g. through fees or commissions.
  • The project is directly related to and in some way enhances the use of dogecoin.
  • The project can be an entirely new product, or an update to an existing product.

For a person or persons to collect a development bounty, they must meet ALL (5) of the following criteria:

  • Ensure that the product fulfils the project brief described in the listing.
  • Ensure that the product has been tested to an appropriately functional state.
  • Provide proof that you are the developer of the claiming product.
  • Have had the product available to the general public for a period of at least one week to allow the reporting of any serious bugs or issues that would negatively impact users.
  • (If multiple persons are involved in development) Each person provides independent verification of the percentage split of the bounty they have agreed upon.

Promotional Events

A promotional event can be based online or in the real world. For something to be listed as a promotional event, it must meet any ONE (1) of the following criteria:

  • It is a specific advertising campaign for dogecoin in any medium.
  • It is an an event, competition, or raffle, in which dogecoin will be offered as a prize.
    • If it is run external to what would reasonably be assumed to be part of the dogecoin community, dogecoin and the prize should be appropriately advertised in association, with enough information to inform those who are unaware of dogecoin's existence.

Infrastructure Providers

To be listed as an infrastructure provider you must meet BOTH (2) of the following criteria:

  • Run or host a service that is critical to the maintenance and support of the dogecoin network (e.g. hypernodes.)
  • The service does not generate a direct income for the owner (e.g. mining pools).

FAQ

What is dogecoin?

Dogecoin is a new form of cryptocurrency similar to bitcoin that was created in late 2013.

How do I get started using dogecoin?

Simply head over to dogecoin.com and download the free wallet app to start being able to send and receive dogecoin. There you will also find many links containing information about the coin and community.

Will dogecoin make me rich?

While this is not a question anyone can truthfully answer, we hope that most people who use Dogecoin do not do so primarily in expectation of this. There are countless other coins on the market that exist as "pump and dump" mechanisms and we wish to differentiate ourselves from this practice.

The point of any currency is to exchange it for goods and services so making a pile and hiding it in a secret lair is no good to anyone. We hope that through this foundation we can encourage the growth and use of dogecoin as the premier currency of the internet, rather than it existing as a fiat equivalent commodity.

So what's the deal with this foundation then?

The Dogecoin Foundation was created to promote use of the currency through a combination of goodwill, charitable, and promotional endeavors, and providing incentives to those with the skills to improve the experience of using the coin.

The members of the dogecoin community have proven themselves time and time again to be a wonderful and generous bunch of people. From assisting new shibes getting set up to banding together to help victims of dishonest opportunists, they have always been forthcoming with aid. We aim to foster that environment by providing a trustworthy and transparent service in order to serve the dogecoin community to the best of our ability.

Who made the Dogecoin Foundation? Who runs it?

The Dogecoin Foundation was conceived by the original creators of dogecoin, BillyM2K (Billy Markus) and ummjackson (Jackson Palmer). The running of the foundation is open to input and management from all members of the core dogecoin team, co-ordinated by the foundation team leader.
